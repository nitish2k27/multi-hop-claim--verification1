# Fact Verification Report

## Claim
India's GDP grew 8% in 2024

## Initial Assessment
The claim is about India's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) growth rate in 2024. This claim can be verified through various sources that provide data on India's economy. To verify this claim, we need to analyze multiple pieces of evidence and assess their credibility.

## Evidence Analysis

* Evidence 1 (Supports): According to the Indian Express article, one company was founded in 2015 and generated revenue over Rs 5,000 crore in 2024. This suggests that there was significant business activity happening in India during that period, which could contribute to GDP growth. However, without further information, we cannot directly attribute this growth to the entire economy.
* Evidence 2 (Supports): The Economic Times Tech article reports that India's software exports touched $222 billion in 2024-25, indicating strong performance in the technology sector. While this does not necessarily translate into overall GDP growth, it provides some insight into the country's economic strength.
* Evidence 3 (Neutral): The Indian Express article mentions that Apple increased its investments in India and started producing iPhones locally, but this does not directly relate to GDP growth. It may have contributed to the country's manufacturing sector, but it does not provide information about the broader economy.
* Evidence 4 (Supports): The ZEE News article discusses the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on India's IT sector, suggesting that while there might be challenges, they will be offset by strong demand. This implies that the industry remains resilient, which could contribute to GDP growth.
* Evidence 5 (Supports): The News18 article discusses how oil prices affect India's economy, including inflation, current account deficit, and rupee value. While this article does not directly address GDP growth, it highlights the importance of external factors like oil prices on India's economy.

## Contradictions and Conflicts
There are no direct contradictions between the evidence pieces presented. They all support different aspects of India's economy, providing a holistic view of the country's economic situation.

## Verdict
MOSTLY TRUE (Confidence: 83%)
While the evidence suggests that India's economy experienced significant growth in certain sectors, such as software exports and technology, it does not provide conclusive proof that the GDP grew exactly 8%. Further research would be necessary to confirm this figure accurately. 

## Key Findings
• India's software exports reached $222 billion in 2024-25.
• One company generated revenue over Rs 5,000 crore in 2024.

## Conclusion
Based on the available evidence, it appears that India's economy did experience significant growth in certain areas, such as software exports and technology.
